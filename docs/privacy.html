WhatsApp Quick Link Native — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-20

This extension does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data about you on any server we control. We have no servers and no analytics.

What the extension does

  • Scans the visible text of web pages you visit to find phone-number-shaped strings.
  • For each candidate number, sends a single HTTP GET request to https://wa.me/<number> (operated by WhatsApp/Meta) to determine whether the number is registered with WhatsApp.
  • If the number is valid, replaces the on-page text with a clickable wa.me link.
  • Caches the validation result locally in your browser (via chrome.storage.local) for up to 30 days, so the same number is not re-checked repeatedly.

What the extension does not do

  • It does not send any data to servers operated by the extension author.
  • It does not include analytics, telemetry, error reporting, or tracking SDKs.
  • It does not read, transmit, or store any content from web pages other than the phone numbers themselves.
  • It does not access your WhatsApp account, contacts, chats, or any authenticated state.
  • It does not sell, share, or transfer data to third parties.

Permissions explained

  • storage — local cache of phone-validation results and your on/off + per-site preferences.
  • host_permissions for wa.me and api.whatsapp.com — making the validation HTTP requests.
  • Content scripts on all sites — phone numbers can appear on any website, so the scanner needs to run on the pages you visit.

Third parties

Validation requests are made to wa.me, operated by WhatsApp LLC / Meta Platforms. Those requests are subject to WhatsApp's privacy policy. We do not control what WhatsApp logs about these requests; the requests originate from your browser's IP address and contain no identifying information beyond what any HTTP client would send.

Changes

This policy may be updated. The "Last updated" date above will change accordingly. Material changes will be reflected in the extension's release notes.
